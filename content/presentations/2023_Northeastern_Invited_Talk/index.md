# Abstract
abstract:  This presentation explores how liquid variation in Spanish (*laterals and rhotics*) interacts with five other sociolinguistic variables among Spanish speakers in Greater Boston. Preliminary results show that highly salient features like liquid variation tend to persist across generations rather than converge with English, highlighting the role of salience in shaping dialectal variation and linguistic coherence.
